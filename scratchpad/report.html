Why it can't happen again

Four independent guards now stand between a stray tab and your rates. A wipe has to defeat all four.

Client · mount
A component's first-render value is never pushed to the cloud. Only genuine later edits sync.
Client · global
Rates / users / clients writes are held until the first full cloud load finishes; anything edited in that window is re-queued by a recovery pass, so nothing is lost.
Server · F20
A write that would replace real rate content — measured across assignments + every profile's services / surcharges + cost tables, not just base costs — with an empty default is refused and snapshotted.
Server · backups
Every overwrite of a critical store snapshots the current value first (newest 10 kept). Total wipes and severe shrinks are refused outright.

Your box catalog got the same protection — an empty or seed boxes write can no longer erase a populated catalog. Shipped as v736 — Ironclad rate/settings wipe guards, the most important change in the batch.

Remaining real gaps, ranked — none on fire; I'd do 1 and 2 next:

  2. Returns don't restock automatically. A return on the shipping side doesn't yet add the item back into WMS on-hand.

  4. No single inventory ledger. Nothing shows every reason a number moved — sold, shipped, received, counted, returned — in one trustworthy view.
Pick lists & packing slips — how to tie them together

Shipping side: the Pick list / Packing slip buttons are stateless print helpers — aggregate the current batch, print, done. Nothing tracked after.

Warehouse side: a WMS pick list is a tracked workflow object — created, assigned, scanned item-by-item, then pack-verified, with a status and history.

So today they share the paper (same document look) but not the process (only WMS tracks who picked what, when). That's the disconnect you're feeling.

Recommended fix: when WMS mode is on, make the shipping-side "Pick list" button create a WMS pick-list object instead of just printing — so a batch queued to ship becomes a real, trackable pick task, and pack-verify closes the loop back to the label. One pipeline: batch → tracked pick/pack → label prints → stock decrements. A focused next project; I didn't touch a live workflow the same night rates were at risk.

Two calls you asked me to make

Warehouse as a free offer?

Yes — as a free, read-only "explore" mode, not the full thing switched on. Flipping the whole WMS on for everyone would slow their app and let them change stock that then fights your assumptions. Better: leave the Warehouse tab visible to everyone in a sample/explore state (it already ships a sample loader and guided tour) with a clear "Turn on WMS for my account" call-to-action. They see the value and self-qualify; you keep control of who runs live inventory. It's a feature-flag change — show the tab, gate the live data behind the existing switch. Low risk, high "oh, this is here?" value.

Dropoffs & Mailboxes — do you need them?

Both are retail pack-and-ship counter tools (think a UPS-Store-style front desk):

  • Dropoffs — an intake log for packages a customer physically hands you to ship; move each pending → shipped. Only useful if you take walk-in packages over a counter.
  • Mail Boxes — a rental registry: who rents which physical mailbox, contact, status (active / vacant / overdue). Only useful if you rent out mailboxes.

If your business is 3PL / ecommerce fulfillment, they're clutter for that audience. If any client runs a retail shipping storefront, they're exactly right. My call: keep them behind a "Front-desk tools" toggle that's off by default, so they only appear for the counter-type clients who want them.

What I deliberately did not do
  • Deep code-split of the app bundle. The real long-term win for load speed is splitting the 17k-line bundle. I did the safe fix (instant cache-first paint) but held the split — it's a large refactor, and doing it the same night your rates were at risk was the wrong trade. Right next performance project when there's time to test it properly.
  • Auto-linking pick-list workflows and returns-restock (gaps 1 & 2). Both change live workflows; better done deliberately than overnight.
